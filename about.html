About the Project

AI Stock Price Prediction
Using BiLSTM + FinBERT

A hybrid deep learning and NLP approach to forecasting next-day stock movement across large-cap technology equities listed on NASDAQ.

Background

Problem & Approach

The Problem

Stock price movement is driven by more than historical prices. News events, earnings surprises, executive statements, and macro narratives all move markets — yet most prediction models rely on technical indicators alone.

This creates a gap: sentiment-driven moves are systematically missed by price-only models, leaving a meaningful signal on the table.

Our Approach

We designed a hybrid pipeline that fuses two complementary signals: BiLSTM for learning temporal price patterns, and FinBERT for extracting daily sentiment from financial news.

The combined feature vector is used to predict whether each stock will close UP or DOWN on the next trading day — a binary directional classification task.

Methodology

End-to-end pipeline

From raw market data and news articles to a live prediction interface — five stages, built for reproducibility.

01
Data collection
OHLCV price data and financial news headlines pulled from multiple public APIs. Reddit and StockTwits were evaluated but excluded due to rate limits and API access restrictions.
yfinanceFinnhubAlpha VantageNewsAPIGDELT
02
FinBERT sentiment scoring
8,444 financial news articles processed through a FinBERT transformer model. Each article is classified as Positive, Negative, or Neutral with a confidence score, then aggregated to a daily per-stock sentiment value.
Positive: 2,565Negative: 3,662Neutral: 1,808
03
Feature engineering & normalisation
Technical indicators (RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, volume ratios) computed from daily price data. Merged with sentiment scores and normalised using MinMaxScaler before training.
RSIMACDBollinger BandsVolume ratioSentiment score
04
BiLSTM model training
A Bidirectional LSTM network trained on chronologically split data — no data leakage. Processes rolling windows of fused price and sentiment features to predict next-day directional movement.
PyTorchChronological splitGoogle Colab
05
Web application deployment
A React + FastAPI web app allows users to select a stock, trigger live BiLSTM inference, and view results alongside a FinBERT sentiment gauge — hosted on OCI Cloud (Always Free ARM instance).
